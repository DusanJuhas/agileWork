Scrum essentials — artifacts & commitments

Scrum Artifacts

Artifact

Product Backlog

Owner: Product Owner

An ordered, evolving list of everything needed in the product. It is the single source of work for the Scrum Team.

  • Dynamic; refined continuously as market, product, and technology change.
  • Items ordered by value, risk, dependencies, and strategy.
  • Contains features, enhancements, bug fixes, technical work, and research.
Artifact

Sprint Backlog

Owner: Developers

Selected Product Backlog items for the current Sprint, plus a plan for delivering the Increment and achieving the Sprint Goal.

  • Created during Sprint Planning; updated throughout the Sprint.
  • Shows work and actionable tasks to meet the Sprint Goal.
  • Highly visible, reflecting current status and daily insights.
Artifact

Increment

Purpose: Usable, potentially shippable product

The sum of all completed Product Backlog items that meet the Definition of Done. Each Increment adds to prior increments and must be in a releasable state.

  • Meets the team's Definition of Done (DoD).
  • May be released at the end of the Sprint or at any time.
  • Provides transparency for inspection and adaptation.
