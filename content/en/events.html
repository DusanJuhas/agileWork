SCRUM Events

SCRUM defines five key events that structure the framework and ensure transparency, inspection, and adaptation:

1. Sprint

  • Description: The core time-boxed iteration where work is done to create a potentially shippable product increment.
  • Duration: Typically 1–4 weeks.
  • Purpose: Provides a consistent rhythm for development and delivery.

2. Sprint Planning

  • Description: A meeting at the start of the Sprint where the team decides what will be delivered and how it will be achieved.
  • Participants: Scrum Team (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers).
  • Outputs: Sprint Goal, Sprint Backlog.

3. Daily Scrum

  • Description: A short, daily meeting (15 minutes) for Developers to synchronize activities and plan for the next 24 hours.
  • Focus: Progress toward the Sprint Goal and any impediments.

4. Sprint Review

  • Description: Held at the end of the Sprint to inspect the increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed.
  • Participants: Scrum Team + Stakeholders.
  • Purpose: Gather feedback and discuss what’s next.

5. Sprint Retrospective

  • Description: A meeting after the Sprint Review for the Scrum Team to reflect on the past Sprint and identify improvements.
  • Focus: Process, tools, collaboration, and team dynamics.
